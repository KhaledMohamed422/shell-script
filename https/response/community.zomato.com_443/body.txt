The inspiration came from the farm-to-table concept, and Executive Chef Sean MacDougall and his team highlighted a quintessential marriage between Indonesian farmers&rsquo; freshest ingredients and food artisans&rsquo; creativity. The buffet exhibited the union uniquely, in a series of tantalizing international selections, friendly local flavors and the fusion of the two. To celebrate this, Pasola invited <b><a href="https://href.li/?http://www.zomato.com" target="_blank">Zomato</a></b> and Zomato foodies to enjoy a range of delightful appetizers, main courses, pass around and desserts.<b><br /></b></p><figure class="tmblr-full" data-orig-height="768" data-orig-width="1024"><img src="https://64.media.tumblr.com/1793cefab5a4b47f162a00a70c02dc2a/44875218621daa03-ad/s500x750/5c92bc9feb97c324f89969e526cf82d993540e1f.jpg" data-orig-height="768" data-orig-width="1024" width="500" height="375" alt="image" /></figure><p>What a lucky day for us! It was a cozy Sunday morning, and <b>Zomato</b> and <b>Zomato foodies</b> were pampered with appetizers of oysters, scallops and prawns laid out on ice, as well as premium-grade sushi and sashimi. In contrast to the cold, Pasola offered a series of hot dishes, cooked live in front of patrons. On to the European dishes, the trained chefs teamed up to serve Roast Dijon Rib Eye Steak, Lamb Rack and Mini Foie Gras Beef Burger, as well as the lighter selection of Eggs Benedict with Spinach and Salmon. We were quite filled with them, but we were still excited to try the East Asian dishes. Commencing the Asian taste with an assortment of freshly-steamed Dim Sum and newly-fried Tempura, we moved on to savoring their authentic Peking Duck. The tender and flavorful duck meat was wrapped in thin and crisp skins, where every bite gave a juicy, yet crunchy sensation. Better yet, the chef sliced the meat on demand, right in front of us! Only the freshest from Pasola, as we were told. Our group then moved on to pamper our local taste buds. We started off with Barramundi Pesmol, baked fish covered in savory and sour turmeric sauce, followed by Soto Sum Sum, a bone marrow dish immersed in garlic, onion and shallot-seasoned soup. The last entr&eacute;e was our ever-familiar Pempek Palembang, a Sumatran-originated dish, where the springy fish cake was fried into perfection and served alongside refreshingly-sour but sweetly-balancing sauce. As if we had not eaten enough, we were then in the last station; Desserts! Those with sweet teeth - basically each and every one of us, we were first introduced to the chocolate-based desserts, starting with&nbsp; Churros and Chocolate Sauce, Bali Chocolate Slice with Honeycomb, Warm Chocolate Melt with Ice Cream, even Chocolate Fountain! Seriously, who could resist fresh fruits dipped in chocolate fountains? We certainly could not! Balancing out the decadence of chocolate, we were then pampered with a fusion tangy dessert, Harum Manis Mango Mousse, followed by Strawberry Jelly with White Chocolate Rocks and a station of Homemade Gelato in different flavors.&nbsp;</p><figure class="tmblr-full" data-orig-height="768" data-orig-width="1024"><img src="https://64.media.tumblr.com/ea52b201fbf591ad872518e79be7d4e4/44875218621daa03-f6/s500x750/c4438a24e9e22557dc797bcd8520ff5b5b9985fb.jpg" data-orig-height="768" data-orig-width="1024" width="500" height="375" alt="image" /></figure><p><a href="https://href.li/?https://www.zomato.com/jakarta/pasola-the-ritz-carlton-pacific-place-scbd" target="_blank">Pasola</a> offered us a full-stop meal experience, and it was one neither Zomato and our foodies would never forget!
